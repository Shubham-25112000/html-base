Fundamental Design Principles

üéØ 1. Hierarchy

Guide users' attention through size, color, and positioning. Most important elements should be most prominent.

‚öñÔ∏è 2. Balance

Distribute visual weight evenly. Use symmetrical or asymmetrical balance to create harmony.

üîÑ 3. Consistency

Maintain consistent patterns in colors, fonts, spacing, and interactions throughout the design.

üé® 4. Contrast

Use differences in color, size, or style to make important elements stand out and improve readability.

üï≥Ô∏è 5. White Space

Empty space is powerful. It improves readability, creates focus, and gives designs room to breathe.

Accessibility & Usability

‚ôø Accessibility Checklist:

  • Color Contrast: Minimum 4.5:1 ratio for normal text
  • Font Size: At least 16px for body text
  • Focus States: Visible keyboard navigation
  • Alt Text: Descriptive text for images
  • Screen Readers: Proper heading hierarchy
  • Touch Targets: Minimum 44px for mobile buttons

Usability Heuristics:

üîç Visibility of System Status

Always inform users about what's happening through feedback

üó£Ô∏è Match Real World

Use familiar language and concepts from users' world

üéÆ User Control

Provide undo/redo options and clear exits

üìè Consistency

Follow platform conventions and maintain consistency
